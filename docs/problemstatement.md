# Problem Statement: Understanding Barriers to Music Discovery at Spotify

## Background

Spotify has successfully acquired millions of users and built one of the world's most sophisticated music recommendation ecosystems. Through personalized playlists, algorithmic recommendations, editorial curation, radio stations, and search experiences, Spotify helps users access an enormous catalog of music tailored to their tastes.

Despite these capabilities, a significant portion of listening activity remains concentrated around familiar artists, previously discovered tracks, saved libraries, and repeat playlists. While repeat listening contributes to engagement and user satisfaction, it can also indicate that users are not consistently discovering and adopting new music.

Increasing meaningful music discovery is a strategic priority for Spotify because discovery drives long-term engagement, broadens listening habits, increases exposure to emerging artists, and strengthens the value of the platform's recommendation ecosystem.

However, Spotify currently has limited visibility into the underlying reasons why users struggle to discover new music and why repetitive listening behaviors persist. Behavioral analytics can reveal what users are listening to, but often fail to explain the motivations, frustrations, and unmet needs behind those behaviors.

Large volumes of user feedback exist across reviews, community discussions, and social conversations, but these signals are fragmented, unstructured, and difficult to analyze systematically at scale.

## Problem

Spotify lacks a comprehensive understanding of the factors that prevent users from successfully discovering new music and engaging with recommendations beyond their existing listening habits.

As a result, product teams face challenges in answering critical questions about user discovery experiences, recommendation effectiveness, and evolving listener needs.

Without a structured understanding of user feedback, Spotify risks:

* Continuing to serve recommendations that feel repetitive or predictable.
* Missing emerging user frustrations related to discovery experiences.
* Failing to identify unmet needs that could drive future product innovation.
* Overlooking differences in discovery challenges across user segments.
* Making product decisions based primarily on behavioral signals without understanding user intent and perception.

## Scope of Investigation

To better understand the music discovery problem, Spotify wants to analyze user-generated feedback from the following sources:

### App Store Reviews

User reviews submitted through the Apple App Store that discuss recommendation quality, discovery experiences, feature requests, satisfaction, and frustrations.

### Google Play Store Reviews

User feedback from Android listeners highlighting pain points, expectations, and challenges related to discovering new music.

### Reddit Discussions

Organic discussions where users openly share opinions, experiences, complaints, and recommendations regarding Spotify's discovery and recommendation ecosystem.

### Community Forums

Discussions within Spotify's community forums where users raise issues, suggest improvements, and discuss discovery-related experiences.

### Social Media Conversations

Public conversations that provide real-time insights into user sentiment, emerging trends, expectations, and unmet needs related to music discovery.

## Key Questions to Answer

### 1. Why do users struggle to discover new music?

Identify and understand the primary barriers preventing successful music discovery.

Examples may include:

* Repetitive recommendations.
* Lack of genre diversity.
* Difficulty finding new or emerging artists.
* Over-personalization.
* Limited exploration capabilities.
* Poor recommendation relevance.

### 2. What are the most common frustrations with recommendations?

Understand recurring complaints and dissatisfaction drivers related to Spotify's recommendation experience.

Examples may include:

* Recommendations feeling repetitive.
* Irrelevant suggestions.
* Lack of novelty.
* Similar-sounding recommendations.
* Poor adaptation to changing tastes.
* Recommendation fatigue.

### 3. What listening goals are users trying to accomplish?

Understand the underlying needs and motivations behind listening behavior.

Examples may include:

* Discovering new artists.
* Exploring new genres.
* Finding music for a specific mood or activity.
* Staying current with new releases.
* Discovering niche or underground music.
* Receiving unexpected but relevant recommendations.

### 4. What drives repetitive listening behavior?

Understand why users repeatedly return to the same artists, playlists, and tracks instead of exploring new content.

Potential drivers may include:

* Familiarity and comfort.
* Habit formation.
* Trust in known content.
* Lack of confidence in recommendations.
* Limited time available for exploration.
* Fear of investing time in poor recommendations.

### 5. How do discovery challenges vary across user segments?

Identify whether different user groups experience distinct discovery-related challenges.

Potential segments include:

* New vs. existing users.
* Free vs. Premium subscribers.
* Casual vs. power listeners.
* Different age groups.
* Different geographic markets.
* Genre-specific audiences.

### 6. What unmet needs consistently emerge across user feedback?

Identify recurring requests, gaps, and opportunities that are not adequately addressed today.

Examples may include:

* Greater control over recommendations.
* Better discovery tools.
* Improved transparency into recommendation logic.
* Enhanced social discovery experiences.
* More diverse recommendation pathways.
* Better support for niche music exploration.

## Desired Outcomes

Spotify wants to develop a deeper understanding of the user experience surrounding music discovery and recommendation consumption.

Specifically, the investigation should:

* Identify the primary barriers to successful music discovery.
* Quantify the most common recommendation-related frustrations.
* Understand the motivations behind listening behaviors.
* Explain the drivers of repetitive listening patterns.
* Reveal differences in discovery experiences across user segments.
* Surface recurring unmet needs and emerging opportunities.
* Establish a data-backed understanding of how users perceive Spotify's discovery ecosystem.

## Success Criteria

The investigation will be successful if it enables Spotify to:

* Clearly articulate the root causes of poor discovery experiences.
* Prioritize the most significant discovery-related pain points.
* Understand how user needs differ across listener segments.
* Detect emerging trends in user sentiment and expectations.
* Generate evidence-based opportunities for improving music discovery.
* Support future product strategy and experimentation with user-backed insights.

## Summary

Spotify's recommendation systems have achieved significant scale and sophistication, yet many users continue to rely heavily on familiar content and repeat listening behaviors. To improve meaningful music discovery, Spotify must first understand the underlying motivations, frustrations, barriers, and unmet needs that shape user behavior.

By systematically analyzing user feedback across app reviews, community forums, Reddit discussions, and social media conversations, Spotify aims to develop a comprehensive understanding of discovery challenges and identify opportunities to improve the overall music discovery experience.
