What are the most common frustrations with recommendations?

auto_awesome

summarize Synthesized Answer

The overwhelming majority of user frustration stems from a perceived mismatch between algorithmic suggestions and actual user intent. Users consistently report that recommendations feel "random" or fail to account for their current mood, often suggesting content based on isolated past interactions rather than broader listening habits.

A secondary, yet significant, theme involves the lack of user control over the recommendation engine. Users express annoyance at the inability to 'dislike' or actively filter out specific genres or artists that have incorrectly dominated their discovery feeds. This leads to a feeling of being trapped by the algorithm's assumptions.
